Maybe I have brought up some hard memories for you to dwell on. However, I know from experience it is better to remember something, even bad memories, than nothing at all. Everything I can remember allows me to heal personally and learn something.

At the time this was written you had many problems. Panic attacks, anxiety, stress 24/7, compulsive scratching, hyper- ventilating, etc. You couldn't name a single thing you enjoyed/appreciated about yourself. Please, think about it. List the things you like about yourself and dwell on that. Would you rather be happy or spend all your time trying to conform to society's pathetic standards?

Since I doubt that anyone I should ever see again let me remind you of things other people said they loved about you. Your eyes. Eyes that turn bright turquoise when you cry, but usually a gray-blue. On some ocassions they would be gray or even green. You had significantly larger pupils than most--that or they were always dilated. It gave you a inquisitive, intelligent look. Your hair. You never had to straighten it. The most you would do is brush it and go to school with it wet. It would stay straight every time. Even when you would try to curl it, it would go back to straight within the hour. Your wisdom. More than once people have told you that you are wise beyond your years. Maturity. Same thing, you have always been very mature for your age.

Dwell on those things! Don't let other people get you down. They set their standards high because they think poorly of themselves. Encourage them, even those who are mean to you. Smile, give compliments, make eye contact and keep your chin up. It will do miracles.

Your family loves you, your horse adores you, the doggies can't stand it when you leave, and most of God loves you.

I pray to God that by now you have learned to love yourself.Good luck, old friend.
