The Luxe Series

It's a series of four books set in Manhatten in 1899. It's a romantic comedy with no action but I still like it. It's wierd for me to like something like that. I usually read things that have action in them, but I still liked this and there was no action invovled. Rumors was my favorite and I haven't read Envy yet. I've discovered that I can't read them in quick sucession because I'll get either too invovled or end up hating the books. I haven't touched them in about two years. I really should get on that since the fourth one is out.

Star Wars Episode 1 - The Phantom Menace

The one everyone over looks constantly. People always say this was the worst of the movies. I say "Let's focus on the good things"!

  • New Planets - The galaxy's not limited to Alderaan, Hoth, Yavin, Tatooine, and Endor anymore. There's Couruscant, Naboo, and the return of Tatooine. It was the first glimps of a larger galaxy before the revised ROTJ came out. It was awesome.

  • Jedi - More Jedi! That's cool. They have their own system. We can see how they worked before the Empire rose. I don't know about you, but I found it was interesting.

  • Jar Jar Binks - People are always saying how annoyed with him they got. But he was important, if obnoxious. He took Obi-Wan and Qui-Gon to Theed, he got Padm� in to ask for the Gugans help. He was also a stress releaver. And, if he hadn't been in the first movie, they would've had a continuity fail when they had him vote the Executive powers to the Chancellor.

  • Young Obi-Wan - I like Ewan McGregor. He did excellent job as the younger Obi-Wan. He is someone you could see growing up to be Alec Guinness. Plus, now all the Star Wars junkies have someone to tie up in their mediphorical closet. That includes me.
