My favorite book of all time is probably "The Invention of Hugo Cabret" by Brian Selznick.

This is the EXTREMELY MAGICAL tale of Hugo Cabret, an orphan living alone in the walls of the Paris train station durring the 1930s. A few years earlier, Hugo's father was killed when a fire engulfed the museum he worked at, forcing Hugo to live with his drunken, and frequently absent Uncle Cluade, who works as the clock keeper at the Train Station. But, when Uncle Claude goes missing, Hugo must fend for himself in the station.

He encounters a broken down man known as Papa Georges, and his god-daughter, Isabell, and together, the two (Hugo and Isabelle), embark on an AMAZING journey to uncopver a message from Hugo's father, which is hidden in a automation, a robotic man. They also uncover a secret from Papa Georges' past, and set out to restore the man to his former glory.

Why do I love this book? It's use of sketches and words to tell it's magical story. Brian Selznick's sketches are possibly the most beautiful ones I have ever seen. I ADORE THIS BOOK!!!!!!
