WHAT IS FREEDOM TO YOU? WHAT DEFINES YOU TO BE TRULY "FREE"?


Freedom, (to me), isn't some word that can be caged in labels like "the power to act", or "right to think/speak,etc.". It can't be rolled over like trash from the higher-ups, and locked away forever because most think it will endanger all of society". That's just selfish thinking. For what they think is chaos, I believe is order.


Think about it.


When we are born and we take in our first painful ounce of life, we feel alive. Free, almost because we've escaped from our mother's womb to experience what the outside world holds for us. Sure, after so those many years of coddling and teaching are drilled into our minds, we feel trapped again. Almost like our "wings" have been clipped for the second time in order to prevent us from taking off into a world unknown. But in those painful attempts to fly again, we begin to hope; we begin to believe. We take for granted what freedom can feel lke. It can feel like a million hearts ar beating within one body. It can feel like the times that were good, and sometimes the bad.


Freedom isn't the word that you can easily search for in a dictionary. Maybe it isn't even what I believe. To me, freedom is the writhing, caged animal ready to take flight.

Define "happiness"


Happiness? Well, I'm not quite sure about what happiness truly is since I've never experienced it full on. Sure, getting the things you want and actually enjoying them for a while could seem like happiness... but is it really? I mean, we take for granted what we believe is really happiness. Things like getting a job after so long, finding the "right one", buying that new game system, etc., etc.
But those are just wants, and temporary spikes. Not real happiness. If I were to summarize it, I'd say happiness is that feeling when your soul; your inner self, knows that what is going on is making you smile.
