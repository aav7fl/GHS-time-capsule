The Leatt GPX Club neck brace system is one of my items for many reasons. The Leatt brace has been proven to save lives and prevent severe neck and spinal injuries. The brace is becoming one of the must haves for riding motocross these days due to people going faster and jumping higher and bigger jumps. I wear one so I dont become a vegetable in the rare case of a severe crash.They are wore by many pros and amatuers today and they just came out two years ago. They are expensive ranging from the Adventure at $285 to the Club which is $395. However, what would you rather do, spend money on something that will save your life or be a vegetable?




The 2010 CRF450R made by Honda is in my time capsule becuase it is the definition of the latest and greatest in motocross technology. The 2010 CRF450R has an electronical fuel injection system on it instead of a mechanical one. The 450 comes with a little computer which allows you to tune the jetting of the bike depending on conditions of the track and weather. This bike can also hook directly into a computer via USB allowing you to tune the jetting and motor characteristics from there. This computerisation has eliminated the need for changing out jets or fussing with the air/fuel mixture screw. This bike has won many awards for its inovative design. Plus the bike just flat out RIPS!!!!


The 2010 KTM SX150 is in my time capsule because it is one of the few surviving two-stroke race bikes in a class dominated by four-strokes. The bike is a liquid cooled 150cc engine that hangs with any four-stroke power plant. The bike has power everywhere and the throttle response is crisp all around. This bike is keeping the pre-mix junkies happy and the two stroke supporters confident while racing the four stroke bikes.
