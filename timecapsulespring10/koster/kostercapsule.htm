Hey, I'm Nicholas Koster and this the Time Capsule I made for Mr. Dolloff's 5th Hour Intro to Web Design class. It's finally open after 10 years.



This is just a look back at where I was shortly before Graduation. I had a great time at GHS, but I'm ready to move on to bigger things. I'm going to Western Michigan University. I will be 18 when the Capsule is open for viewing. Look to the right.


























A great way to have fun was my church's youth group, the TYDE. We had a lot of fun chilling out like playing video games, 4-square, BodyBody, Red Ball, PopPong, pool, air hockey, table tennis, and foosball. We discussed God. A running gag was Nestle's Chunkys. Basically, nobody at the TYDE likes them because the chocolate isn't really good and we all think it's weird of combining chocolate with nuts and raisins. I guess it's not that bad of an idea, but the Cunky isn't really good. So whenever a Chunky appeared, it'd get thrown. One time we had a Chunky Chucking Competition.
The world of comic books really got my imagination going. It's kind of crazy, but I think it's so neat how there's this whole different universe. I liked mostly Marvel's superheroes, not DC. My favorites were Wolverine, Spider-Man, Captain America, and Daredevil. It was also exciting to finally see their powers in action on the big screen. Right now in 2010, I am anticipating a long series of movies with all of them interacting together. Maybe when this capsule is unlocked, there will be one in the works finally. Outside of Marvel, I of course liked the funnies too, like Ballard Street, Zits, and Garfield. My best friend in high school really liked comic books too, so this is a representation of him too, kind of.
My first car: an Oldsmobile Bravada. I'm not sure what year it was / is, but who cares? It gets me where I want to go. I guess it'll be important to know its year when it's sold, but right now in 2010 I don't see that happening anytime soon. It doesn't have any miles on it. So far I've been in two accidents. The first time someone backed into me when I wasn't even moving! (I should've honked the horn) But his car, which looked pretty new, got all the damage. The other accident I did the damage. I was parked real to the right in the garage and couldn't quite clear it.
I ran Cross Country all four high school years. Wow, it kind of feels weird to type that. It's really neat for me who's not very athletic, no matter the skill level, everyone is on the same playing field at once. It's really the only sport where the average are with the elite. That's not true in sports like basketball and baseball. I hope I'm still running distance sometime in the future. Everyone on the team was real nice too. I'm not very good at sprinting.
